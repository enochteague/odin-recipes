One of my favorite dishes of all time has got to be pork ramen. The filling, delicious meat is just the perfect combo with the broth, making a very hearty meal! This is a big one, but so worth it.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2.2 lbs rolled pork shoulder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 8 1/2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 onion, cut in half, no need to peel
  • 2 carrots, peeled. One whole, the other cut into matchsticks
  • 1 stick of celery, broke in half
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped in half, no need to peel
  • 1 thumb sized piece of ginger, roughly chopped, no need to peel
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Gochujang Paste
  • 1 red chilli, rougly sliced. Remove seeds if too hot
  • 4 large eggs
  • 7 oz dried ramen noodles
  • 1 leek
  • 3 packed cups baby spinach leaves
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black sesame seeds
  • Small bunch of scallions, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli flakes

Directions!

  1. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees F or 150 degrees C. Put 1 tablespoon of oil in a casserole pan and heat until very hot.
  2. Season the pork with the salt and pepper and place in the pan. Seal on all sides.
  3. Pour the stock over the pork.
  4. Add in the onion, the whole carrot, celery, garlic, and ginger
  5. Now add the mirin, soy sauce, Gochujang, and red chilli. Bring to a boil, then place a lid on the pan and bake for 4 hours. Check multiple times and top with boiling water as needed.
  6. Take the pan out and place the pork on a chopping board. Remove and discard fat, then shred.
  7. Strain broth into a large bowl and discard vegetables (or keep as a yummy treat). Place liquid back in pan with the pork. Leave on low heat to keep warm.
  8. Place the eggs in a small pan and boil for 6 minutes.
  9. Remove eggs from heat and place in cold water
  10. Place the dried noodles in a pan of boiling water and boil for 5 minutes. Strain, rinse, and set aside.
  11. Heat the remaining oil in a frying pan.
  12. Add leek, season with salt and pepper, fry for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  13. Push leeks to the side and place in spinach. Allow it to wilt for 1 minute.
  14. Divide noodles between 4 bowls, top with hot broth, shredded pork, leek, spinach, and carrot matchsticks.
  15. Peel eggs, place in soup.
  16. Garnish with scallion, then sprinkle the sesame seeds and chilli flakes in before serving!
